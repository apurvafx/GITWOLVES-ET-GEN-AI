INCIDENT FAILURE LOG
Incident ID: INC-2026-09
Date of Incident: June 14, 2026
Location: Crude Distillation Unit (CDU-1)
Equipment Affected: PUMP-101A (Refinery Feed Pump)

DESCRIPTION OF EVENT:
At 14:20 hrs, operators received a high-temperature alarm on PUMP-101A bearing housing, showing 88°C (exceeding the OEM thermal threshold limit of 85°C). Within 10 minutes, the temperature reached 92°C, accompanied by excessive pump vibration (registered at 4.2 mm/s, well past the 2.8 mm/s action limit). Before the automatic trip could trigger, a sudden failure occurred at the mechanical seal, causing a seal blowout and a localized hydrocarbon fluid leak. The emergency isolation SOP was initiated, block valves were closed, and electrical VALVE-102 was locked out. The unit was safely shut down.

ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS (RCA):
Subsequent disassembly and inspection of the pump coupling revealed severe shaft misalignment. The coupling misalignment created cyclic fatigue loads on the rotating shaft, causing excessive vibration of 4.2 mm/s. The high vibration degraded the mechanical seal faces and generated friction heat, pushing bearing temperatures to 92°C and resulting in seal blowout. 

CORRECTIVE ACTIONS:
1. Re-align pump shaft using laser alignment system to under 0.05 mm tolerance.
2. Update local maintenance logs for PUMP-101A to require weekly vibration scans.
