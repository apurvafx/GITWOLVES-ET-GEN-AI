# Spare Parts

## Purpose
Defines recommended spare parts inventory, ordering information, storage practice, and required tools for maintaining PUMP-101A.

## Engineering Information
Maintaining correct spare parts inventory for PUMP-101A minimizes downtime during scheduled and unscheduled maintenance. Only genuine OEM replacement parts to the original design specification should be used; any modification or use of a non-standard part invalidates the pump's safety certification.

### Ordering Information Required
1. Pump serial number (from nameplate)
2. Pump size (from nameplate)
3. Part name (from parts list/drawing)
4. Part number (from parts list/drawing)
5. Number of parts required

### Recommended Spares for Two Years' Operation (per VDMA 24296)
Quantities scale with fleet size (number of pumps including standby). Key wear and consumable items include:
- Impeller
- Pump shaft
- Bearing bush (front and rear)
- Tolerance ring
- Sleeve (if fitted)
- Thrust collar
- Gasket – thrust collar
- Drive pin – thrust collar
- Shim pack
- Inner rotor / Outer rotor (magnet assemblies)
- Shell (PEEK or metallic — see containment shell configuration)
- Ball bearing
- Casing gasket
- Shell O-ring set / secondary O-ring
- Remaining gasket set
- Skid ring (if fitted)

For a 2-pump fleet (including standby), typical minimum quantities are 1 each of impeller/shaft/bearing bushes/rotor/shell, 2 of tolerance rings/sleeves/thrust collar items, 4 of casing gaskets, and 2 of shell O-ring sets. Quantities scale up for larger fleets — consult the VDMA 24296 table for fleet sizes of 3 to 10+.

### Tools Required (Standard)
- Open ended spanners up to M24 screws/nuts
- Socket spanners up to M48 screws
- Allen keys up to 10mm (A/F)
- Range of screwdrivers
- Soft mallet

### Tools Required (Specialized)
- Bearing pullers
- Bearing induction heater
- Dial test indicator
- C-spanner (wrench) for removing the shaft nut — order part no. N.919GZ172 (Model 80) or N.919GZ173 (Model 100/150) if not otherwise available

## Procedure

### Storage of Spares
- Store in a clean, dry area away from vibration.
- Inspect and re-treat metallic surfaces with preservative at 6-monthly intervals.

## Inspection Points
- Spare parts inventory versus VDMA 24296 recommended quantities for fleet size.
- Metallic spare part surfaces re-treated with preservative every 6 months.
- Ordering information (serial number, size, part name/number) confirmed against nameplate before ordering.

## Warnings
- Only genuine OEM replacement parts to the original design specification should be used; modification or use of a non-standard part invalidates the pump's safety certification.
- Always confirm the pump serial number and size from the nameplate before ordering spares to avoid receiving incorrect parts.

## Best Practices
- Store spares in a clean, dry area away from vibration, and inspect/re-treat metallic surfaces with preservative every 6 months.
- Keep specialized tools (bearing pullers, induction heater, dial test indicator, C-spanner) on hand before beginning an overhaul to avoid delays.
- Scale spares inventory to actual fleet size per the VDMA 24296 table rather than using single-pump quantities for multi-pump fleets.

### FAQ
Q: What information is required to order spare parts for PUMP-101A?
A: Pump serial number and size (from the nameplate), part name and part number (from the parts list/drawing), and the number of parts required.

Q: Why must only genuine OEM replacement parts be used?
A: Any modification or use of a non-standard part invalidates the pump's safety certification.

Q: What standard is used to determine recommended spares quantities for a pump fleet?
A: VDMA 24296, which scales recommended quantities with fleet size (number of pumps including standby) for two years of operation.

Q: What specialized tool is needed to remove the shaft nut, and what is its part number?
A: A C-spanner (wrench) — part no. N.919GZ172 for Model 80, or N.919GZ173 for Model 100/150.
