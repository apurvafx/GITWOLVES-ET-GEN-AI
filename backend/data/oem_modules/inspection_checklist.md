# Inspection Checklist

## Purpose
Consolidates OEM-recommended inspection points for PUMP-101A across daily/weekly, six-monthly, pre-commissioning, post-piping-connection, and overhaul examination activities.

## Engineering Information
Routine and periodic inspection of PUMP-101A identifies deterioration before it leads to failure. This checklist consolidates OEM-recommended inspection points.

## Inspection Points

### Daily / Weekly Checks
- [ ] Noise level normal
- [ ] Vibration within normal range
- [ ] Bearing temperature normal
- [ ] Oil level and condition acceptable (oil lubricated units)
- [ ] Grease recharge/change interval tracked (grease lubricated units)
- [ ] Auxiliary heating/cooling supplies functioning
- [ ] No visible leakage at casing, gaskets, or shell
- [ ] Shaft turned periodically if idle >2 weeks (ferrous wetted components)

### Six-Monthly Checks
- [ ] Foundation bolts secure and free of corrosion
- [ ] Bearing lubricant change interval reviewed against running hours
- [ ] Coupling alignment verified; driving elements checked for wear
- [ ] Spare parts in storage inspected/re-treated with preservative

### Pre-Commissioning Checks
- [ ] Bearing housing filled to correct oil level or grease pre-fill confirmed
- [ ] Direction of rotation verified with coupling spacer removed
- [ ] Pump casing and suction pipe completely filled with liquid (CPXS/CPXNS) or initial fill added (CPXPS self-priming)
- [ ] All guards and safety devices installed and secure
- [ ] Electrical connections completed by a qualified electrician
- [ ] Emergency stop device fitted and functional

### Post-Piping-Connection Checks
- [ ] Shaft rotates freely by hand with no binding
- [ ] Coupling alignment rechecked for pipe strain; corrected if needed
- [ ] All suction and discharge pipework bolts tight
- [ ] All foundation bolts tight

### Examination of Parts (During Overhaul)
- [ ] Internal parts cleaned thoroughly
- [ ] Wear, pitting, corrosion, erosion, or sealing surface irregularities checked; replace as necessary
- [ ] Wash flow strainer (if fitted) cleaned of debris
- [ ] Lubrication holes in casing cover, magnet carrier, and shaft cleared
- [ ] Injection, vent, drain, and return holes on casing cover inspected/cleaned
- [ ] Balance holes of inner rotor cleared of debris
- [ ] All gaskets and O-rings replaced (not reused)
- [ ] Bearings, shaft, and bearing seals in the driver inspected for contamination/damage

## Warnings
- Never skip the pre-commissioning rotation direction check — the coupling spacer must be removed to verify rotation safely.
- Pumps handling liquid with ferrous wetted components must have the shaft turned periodically if idle more than 2 weeks, to prevent rust/algae buildup.
- All gaskets and O-rings must be replaced (not reused) during overhaul.

## Best Practices
- Track running hours against lubricant change intervals rather than relying on calendar time alone.
- Recheck coupling alignment after piping connection specifically for pipe-strain-induced misalignment, even if alignment was previously correct.
- Inspect and re-treat stored spare parts with preservative at six-monthly intervals.

### FAQ
Q: How often should bearing temperature and vibration be checked under routine inspection?
A: Daily/weekly, as part of routine operating behavior checks.

Q: What must be verified before commissioning regarding rotation direction?
A: Direction of rotation must be verified with the coupling spacer removed, before continuous operation begins.

Q: Should gaskets and O-rings be reused during overhaul?
A: No — all gaskets and O-rings should be replaced, not reused, during overhaul.

Q: Why should the shaft be turned periodically if the pump sits idle more than two weeks?
A: To prevent rust or algae buildup on ferrous wetted components; ensure the casing is full of liquid before turning if the shaft is inaccessible.
