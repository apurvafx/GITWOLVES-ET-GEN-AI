# Shaft Alignment

## Purpose
Describes the procedure and tolerances for checking and correcting shaft alignment between PUMP-101A and its driver before commissioning and after maintenance.

## Engineering Information

PUMP-101A and its driver are factory aligned, but alignment is commonly disturbed during transport or handling. Alignment must always be checked before commissioning. The correct practice is to align the motor to the pump, not the pump to the motor.

### Permissible Misalignment Limits (working temperature)
- Parallel alignment: 0.25 mm (0.010 in.) TIR maximum
- Angular alignment (couplings ≤100 mm flange diameter): 0.3 mm (0.012 in.) TIR maximum
- Angular alignment (couplings >100 mm flange diameter): 0.5 mm (0.020 in.) TIR maximum
- Note: when checking parallel alignment, the Total Indicator Read-out (TIR) shown is twice the actual shaft displacement.

### Best Practice Target
- Parallel: 0.05–0.075 mm (0.002–0.003 in.)
- Angular: 0.05 mm (0.002 in.) per 100 mm of coupling flange diameter

### Soft Foot Tolerance
- Maximum acceptable deflection: 0.05 mm (0.002 in.); any more requires shim correction equal to the measured lift.

## Procedure

1. Isolate the pump and driver electrically and disconnect the half couplings before checking alignment.
2. Achieve alignment by adding/removing shims under the motor feet and moving the motor horizontally.
3. If alignment cannot be achieved by adjusting the motor, reposition the pump and repeat.
4. Use a dial indicator for couplings with narrow flanges.
5. Align in the vertical plane first, then horizontally.
6. For motors with sleeve bearings, align the motor to run on its magnetic centreline (see motor manual). A button screwed into one shaft end is typically used to fix axial position.

### Soft Foot Check
1. Remove all shims and clean surfaces.
2. Tighten the driver to the baseplate.
3. Set a dial indicator on the motor foot.
4. Loosen the holding-down bolt and note deflection.
5. A maximum of 0.05 mm (0.002 in.) is acceptable; any more requires shim correction equal to the measured lift.
6. Repeat on all feet until within tolerance.

### Thermal Expansion Adjustment
Pump and motor are normally aligned at ambient temperature with an allowance for thermal expansion at operating temperature. For high liquid temperature installations, run the unit at actual operating temperature, shut down, and immediately recheck alignment.

### Final Check
After connecting piping, rotate the shaft by hand several times to confirm free movement, then recheck coupling alignment to ensure no pipe strain has been introduced. Correct piping if strain exists.

## Inspection Points
- Parallel and angular misalignment (dial indicator TIR readings).
- Soft foot deflection at each motor foot.
- Free hand rotation of shaft after piping connection.
- Alignment recheck after thermal stabilization for high-temperature service.

## Warnings
- Alignment must always be checked before commissioning, even though the unit is factory aligned — transport and handling commonly disturb it.
- Always align the motor to the pump, not the pump to the motor.
- Pipe strain introduced during piping connection can cause misalignment even if coupling alignment was previously correct — always recheck after piping is connected.
- Electrically isolate the pump and driver, and disconnect the half couplings, before checking or adjusting alignment.

## Best Practices
- Target near-perfect alignment (0.05–0.075 mm parallel, 0.05 mm/100 mm angular) rather than only meeting the maximum permissible limits, for maximum reliability.
- For high-temperature installations, verify alignment at actual operating temperature after thermal stabilization, not only at ambient.
- Perform a soft foot check before final alignment to avoid introducing baseplate stress.
