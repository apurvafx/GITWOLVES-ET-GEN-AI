# Storage

## Purpose
Describes correct receipt, handling, lifting, and storage practices to protect PUMP-101A bearings, seal faces, and casing prior to installation or during extended shutdown.

## Engineering Information
Correct storage practices protect PUMP-101A bearings, seal faces, and casing from damage prior to installation or during extended shutdown periods.

### Lifting Requirements
- A crane must be used for all pump sets exceeding 25 kg (55 lb).
- The angle between two load-carrying slings/ropes must not exceed 60°.

## Procedure

### Short-Term Storage (up to 6 months)
1. Store the pump in a clean, dry location away from vibration.
2. Leave piping connection covers in place to prevent dirt and foreign material from entering the pump casing.
3. Turn the pump shaft at intervals to prevent brinelling of the bearings and sticking of seal faces (if fitted).
4. Consult the OEM for preservative actions if storage will exceed 6 months.

### Spare Parts Storage
- Store spares in a clean, dry area away from vibration.
- Inspect and re-treat metallic surfaces with preservative at 6-monthly intervals.

### Consignment Receipt and Unpacking
1. Check the equipment against delivery/shipping documents for completeness and transport damage immediately upon receipt.
2. Report any shortage or damage to the supplier in writing within one month of receipt; later claims may not be accepted.
3. Check crates, boxes, or wrappings for accessories or spare parts packed separately.
4. Verify the unique serial number matches documentation; always quote this number for correspondence, spares orders, and accessories.

### Handling
Boxes, crates, pallets, or cartons may be unloaded using forklifts or slings depending on size and construction.

### Lifting
1. A crane must be used for all pump sets exceeding 25 kg (55 lb).
2. Only fully trained personnel should perform lifting, in accordance with local regulations.
3. Position slings, ropes, and lifting gear so they cannot slip and a balanced lift is achieved.
4. The angle between two load-carrying slings/ropes must not exceed 60°.
5. Bare pump: lift using the sling arrangement shown in OEM drawings.
6. Pump with folded steel/polycrete baseplate: no dedicated lifting points exist; sling around the discharge nozzle and the outboard motor frame end using choker hitches, keeping weight off the motor fan housing.
7. Pump with cast iron/fabricated baseplate: use the specific lifting points provided.
8. Refer to the driver manufacturer's instructions before lifting the driver alone.

## Inspection Points
- Equipment checked against delivery/shipping documents immediately upon receipt for completeness and transport damage.
- Serial number verified against documentation.
- Pump shaft turned at intervals during short-term storage to prevent bearing brinelling.
- Sling angle verified not to exceed 60° before any lift.

## Warnings
- Report any shortage or damage to the supplier in writing within one month of receipt — later claims may not be accepted.
- A crane must be used for all pump sets exceeding 25 kg (55 lb); only fully trained personnel should perform lifting.
- Keep weight off the motor fan housing when slinging a pump with a folded steel/polycrete baseplate.
- The sling angle between two load-carrying slings/ropes must not exceed 60°.
- For extended shutdowns, especially where ambient temperatures may drop below freezing, drain or otherwise protect the pump and any cooling/flushing arrangements to prevent freeze damage.

## Best Practices
- Leave piping connection covers in place during storage to prevent dirt and foreign material ingress.
- Turn the pump shaft periodically during storage to prevent brinelling of bearings and sticking of seal faces.
- Consult the OEM for additional preservative actions if storage will exceed 6 months.
- Always quote the pump serial number for correspondence, spares orders, and accessories.
