# Fastener Torques

## Purpose
Provides the reference torque specifications for standard screws, the inner rotor locknut, and the magnetic coupling assembly on PUMP-101A, ensuring gasket sealing integrity and preventing joint leakage or component overstress.

## Engineering Information
Correct fastener torque on PUMP-101A ensures gasket sealing integrity, prevents joint leakage, and avoids overstressing casing or cover components.

### Additional Component Torque Values
- Bearing end cover screws: 13 Nm (9.6 lbf·ft), crosswise.
- Outer magnet assembly coupling torque: 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft), crosswise.
- Bearing housing-to-cover bolts: 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft), crosswise.
- Containment shell capscrews (with washers): 11 Nm (8 lbf·ft), crosswise.

## Application Rules
- Non-metallic gaskets undergo creep relaxation — before commissioning, check and retighten fasteners to the stated torques.
- Torque bolts crosswise on all bolting patterns. Do NOT torque in a circular sequence — this causes uneven gasket compression.
- Omitting washers on PEEK containment shell capscrews causes excessive flange damage.
- Inner rotor locknut is left-hand thread; outer rotor and impeller threads are right-hand thread.

## Inspection Points
- Fastener torque values re-checked before commissioning (non-metallic gasket creep relaxation).
- Correct crosswise (star pattern) torque sequence applied on all bolt patterns.
- Washers present on PEEK containment shell capscrews.

## Warnings
- Do NOT torque bolts in a circular sequence — this causes uneven gasket compression and potential leakage.
- Omitting washers on PEEK shell capscrews causes excessive flange damage.
- The inner rotor locknut is a left-hand thread; using right-hand-thread technique risks damaging the assembly.
- Non-metallic gaskets undergo creep relaxation — fasteners must be re-checked and retightened before commissioning.

## Best Practices
- Always retighten fasteners to stated torque values before commissioning, even if they were correctly torqued during assembly, to account for gasket creep relaxation.
- Use a calibrated torque wrench for all specified values, particularly the magnetic coupling factory torque specifications, which are safety- and performance-critical.

### FAQ
Q: Why must fasteners be torqued crosswise rather than in a circular sequence?
A: Circular torque sequencing causes uneven gasket compression, which can lead to leakage; crosswise (star pattern) torque sequencing ensures even, symmetric loading.

Q: What torque is used for the outer magnet assembly coupling?
A: 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft), applied crosswise.

Q: Why must fastener torques be rechecked before commissioning even after assembly?
A: Non-metallic gaskets undergo creep relaxation over time, so fasteners must be checked and retightened to stated torques before commissioning.

Q: What thread direction does the inner rotor locknut use?
A: Left-hand thread, while the outer rotor and impeller threads are right-hand thread.
