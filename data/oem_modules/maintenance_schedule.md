# Maintenance Schedule

## Purpose
Defines the structured maintenance plan — routine, periodic, and re-lubrication intervals — plus lockout/tagout and escalation rules for PUMP-101A.

## Engineering Information
A structured maintenance plan reduces unplanned downtime and preserves the safety certification of PUMP-101A. All maintenance must be performed only by qualified personnel with the machine at a standstill and electrically isolated.

### Operating Parameters Summary
- Sealed-for-life bearings: renew every 12,000 hours running life or every 2 years, whichever is sooner.
- Oil lubricated bearings: change every 4,000 operating hours or at least every 6 months (more frequently for hot/damp/corrosive service).
- Grease lubricated bearings: sealed for life, renew every 12,000 hours or 2 years.
- Idle pumps with ferrous wetted components: turn shaft weekly if idle over 2 weeks.

## Procedure

### Routine Inspection (Daily/Weekly)
- Check operating behaviour: noise, vibration, and bearing temperatures should be normal.
- Check oil lubricant level and condition; for grease lubricated units, check running hours since last recharge.
- Check auxiliary supplies (heating/cooling) are functioning correctly.
- For pumps with ferrous wetted components stood idle over 2 weeks, turn the shaft a few revolutions weekly to break up rust/algae buildup. Ensure the casing is full of liquid before flicking the starter if the shaft is inaccessible.

### Periodic Inspection (Six-Monthly)
- Check foundation bolts for security of attachment and corrosion.
- Review pump running records (hourly usage) to determine if bearing lubricant requires changing.
- Check coupling alignment and worn driving elements; re-align if necessary.

### Re-lubrication
- Oil lubricated bearings: change every 4,000 operating hours or at least every 6 months (more frequently for hot/damp/corrosive service).
- Grease lubricated bearings: sealed for life, renew every 12,000 hours or 2 years.

### General Maintenance Rules
1. Always clean the ground and exterior of the machine before and after maintenance (oil/grease leaks create slip hazards).
2. Place a "Machine under repair: do not start" warning on the starting device.
3. Lock the main switch open and withdraw fuses; tag "Machine under repair: do not connect."
4. Never clean equipment with inflammable solvents or carbon tetrachloride.
5. On completion, reinstall and re-activate all guards and safety devices before restarting.

### Escalation Sequence for Problems
1. Refer to fault diagnosis documentation.
2. Confirm equipment complies with manual recommendations.
3. Contact the OEM/Flowserve if the problem persists.

## Inspection Points
- Noise, vibration, and bearing temperature (daily/weekly).
- Oil level/condition or grease running hours (daily/weekly).
- Foundation bolt security and corrosion (six-monthly).
- Coupling alignment and driving element wear (six-monthly).
- Running hour records versus lubricant change intervals.

## Warnings
- All maintenance must be performed only by qualified personnel with the machine at a standstill and electrically isolated.
- Never clean equipment with inflammable solvents or carbon tetrachloride.
- Lock the main switch open and withdraw fuses; tag "Machine under repair: do not connect" before starting any maintenance work.
- All guards and safety devices must be reinstalled and re-activated before restarting the machine.

## Best Practices
- Track pump running hours to determine actual lubricant change timing rather than relying only on calendar-based schedules.
- Clean the ground and exterior of the machine before and after maintenance to eliminate oil/grease slip hazards.
- Alternate duty/standby pumps to keep both units in good running condition (see running_the_pump.md).
- Escalate unresolved problems through fault diagnosis, then manual compliance verification, then OEM/Flowserve contact.

### FAQ
Q: What is the re-lubrication interval for sealed-for-life bearings?
A: Every 12,000 hours running life or every 2 years, whichever is sooner.

Q: What lockout/tagout steps are required before maintenance?
A: Place a "Machine under repair: do not start" warning on the starting device, lock the main switch open, withdraw fuses, and tag "Machine under repair: do not connect."

Q: Can PUMP-101A be cleaned with inflammable solvents or carbon tetrachloride?
A: No — never clean the equipment with inflammable solvents or carbon tetrachloride.

Q: What is the escalation sequence if a problem persists after maintenance?
A: First refer to fault diagnosis documentation, then confirm the equipment complies with manual recommendations, then contact the OEM/Flowserve if the problem persists.
