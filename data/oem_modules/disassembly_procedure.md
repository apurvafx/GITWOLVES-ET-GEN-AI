# Disassembly Procedure

## Purpose
Describes the correct disassembly sequence for PUMP-101A's back pullout design, allowing the outer or inner assembly to be removed for inspection without disturbing the pump end.

## Engineering Information
PUMP-101A uses a "back pullout" design, allowing the pump casing to remain in line while the outer or inner assembly is removed for inspection of the outer rotor and bearing housing ball bearings, without disturbing the pump end.

### Frame-Specific Jacking Bolt Data
| Frame | Jacking Bolt Size | Extension Distance |
|---|---|---|
| Model 80 | M14×60mm | 32 mm |
| Model 100 | M14×75mm | 53 mm |
| Model 150 | M12×140mm | 105 mm |

- Nominal diametral clearance of outer rotor to skid ring: 1 mm.

## Procedure

### General Precautions
- Lock and tag the power source before starting.
- Ensure the work area is free of grease, oil, and metallic chips/dust (ferritic dust attracts to magnetic assemblies).
- Do not drill, grind, or machine near the work area.
- Confirm genuine OEM replacement parts are available before beginning an overhaul.

### Dismantling the Frame-Mounted Outer Assembly
1. Loosen the bearing housing footbolts.
2. Remove the 4 bolts holding the bearing housing to the casing cover.
3. Insert 2 jacking bolts into the threaded holes in the bearing housing flange (size varies by frame — see table above).
4. Alternately jack the bolts into the flange to release the outer rotor from the flux of the inner rotor (extension distance varies by frame — see table above).
5. Slide the outer assembly out past the shell assembly and secure horizontally.
6. Using a dial indicator, check play of the outer magnet carrier within the bore; if there is contact with the skid ring, the ball bearings must be replaced (nominal diametral clearance of outer rotor to skid ring is 1 mm).

### Removing the Outer Rotor
1. Remove the pipe plug from the side of the housing.
2. Rotate the outer rotor to align its 14mm hole with the plug hole; insert a bar or screw to lock the rotor.
3. Place a coupling hub and key onto the shaft coupling end and loosen the outer magnet assembly (right-hand thread).
4. Unscrew and remove the outer rotor from the bearing housing bore.
5. Light scuffing on the skid ring can be filed; excessive scuffing requires ring removal (saw a cut, lever with a chisel, hammer inward until loose).
6. Remove the bearing end cover screws, remove cover and gasket, and slide the bearing/shaft assembly out.
7. Inspect inboard and outboard ball bearings; replace as necessary.

### Dismantling the Casing Cover Assembly
1. Remove screws holding the casing and casing cover together; pull the cover out and secure horizontally in a vice.
2. Remove containment shell capscrews, containment shell(s), and gasket (discard gasket/O-ring).
3. Loosen and remove the locknut (left-hand thread) using the correct socket/spanner pair.
4. Rotate the inner rotor so the shaft key is at 12 o'clock; slide the inner magnet assembly off and place in a clean, non-ferrous area.
5. Remove the inner magnet assembly, key, and shims.
6. Slide the shaft/impeller assembly out of the casing cover; inspect coated shaft surface or sleeves for damage.
7. Secure the shaft vertically in a vice with soft jaws and loosen the impeller with a strap wrench (right-hand thread).
8. Inspect sleeve bearing bushings; remove only if cracked, chipped, or severely worn (press out using an arbor after removing setscrews if fitted).
9. Remove and discard tolerance rings, thrust collar, and thrust collar gasket.

## Inspection Points
- Outer magnet carrier play in bore (contact with skid ring indicates ball bearings must be replaced).
- Skid ring surface condition (light scuffing can be filed; excessive scuffing requires ring removal).
- Inboard and outboard ball bearing condition.
- Coated shaft surface or sleeve condition.
- Sleeve bearing bushing condition (remove only if cracked, chipped, or severely worn).

## Warnings
- Lock and tag the power source before starting any disassembly work.
- Dismantled magnet assemblies have very strong mutual attraction — handle separately at a safe distance and store in a clean, non-ferrous area.
- Ensure the work area is free of grease, oil, and metallic chips/dust; ferritic dust attracts to magnetic assemblies.
- Do not drill, grind, or machine near the work area.
- Always discard gaskets and O-rings removed during disassembly — do not reuse.

## Best Practices
- Confirm genuine OEM replacement parts are available before beginning an overhaul.
- Jack bolts should be tightened alternately (not fully on one side first) to release the outer rotor evenly.
- Inspect ball bearings, shaft surfaces, and sleeve bushings for damage during every disassembly, even if not scheduled for replacement.

### FAQ
Q: What design feature allows PUMP-101A's outer or inner assembly to be removed without disturbing the pump end?
A: The "back pullout" design, which keeps the pump casing in line while the outer or inner assembly is withdrawn.

Q: How is a worn skid ring identified during disassembly?
A: If the dial indicator shows the outer magnet carrier contacting the skid ring (nominal diametral clearance is 1 mm), the ball bearings must be replaced; light scuffing on the ring itself can be filed, but excessive scuffing requires ring removal.

Q: Why must dismantled magnet assemblies be handled with special care?
A: They have very strong mutual attraction and must be handled separately, at a safe distance, and stored in a clean, non-ferrous area to prevent injury or damage.

Q: What thread direction is used for the outer magnet assembly and inner rotor locknut?
A: The outer magnet assembly uses a right-hand thread; the inner rotor locknut uses a left-hand thread.
