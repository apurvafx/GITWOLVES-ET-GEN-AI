# Nameplate and Name Nomenclature

## Purpose
Explains how to read PUMP-101A's nameplate and model nomenclature, and describes the design of its major parts, to confirm size, ratings, and certification.

## Engineering Information
PUMP-101A's design details, ratings, and certification are engraved on the equipment nameplate. This is the authoritative source for confirming actual pump size, serial number, ATEX classification, and performance limits — always cross-check against applicable certification documents.

### Name Nomenclature Format
Example: `80-50CPXS200`
- First number: nominal suction size in mm
- Second number: nominal discharge size in mm
- Letters: configuration/model code
- Final number: nominal ISO maximum impeller diameter

### Configuration Notes
- Casing feet pads are underneath the casing for the standard and self-priming configurations; on the centreline-mounted variant, feet pads are on the shaft centreline.
- The self-priming configuration's casing incorporates a self-priming action using the reflux principle for suction lifts up to 7 m (23 ft).

### Design of Major Parts
- Pump casing: horizontal centreline end inlet, vertical centreline top outlet (self-venting design); pipe connectors do not need to be disturbed for internal maintenance.
- Impeller: open impeller.
- Shaft: large-diameter stiff shaft on bearings, keyed drive end, fitted with magnetic rotor and product-lubricated bearings.
- Bearing housing: sight glass for oil lubricated bearings; additional lubrication/cooling options may be fitted.
- Pump bearings: ball bearings, oil or grease lubricated; magnetic drive journal bearings lubricated by product or external source.
- Shaft seal: none required — magnetic drive design uses the containment shell between the magnets to prevent leakage.
- Driver: normally an electric motor; alternative drivers (internal combustion engines, turbines, hydraulic motors) may be fitted via couplings, belts, gearboxes, or drive shafts.

## Procedure

### Using the Nameplate
Always verify:
- Pump size and serial number match applicable certification.
- Maximum pump speed rating.
- ATEX classification (if applicable) matches the installation zone.
- CE marking status, if required for the installation region.

## Inspection Points
- Pump size and serial number cross-checked against certification documents.
- ATEX classification versus installation zone.
- Maximum speed rating versus intended operating speed.

## Warnings
- Always cross-check the nameplate against applicable certification documents — the nameplate is the authoritative source for size, serial number, ATEX classification, and performance limits.
- Never exceed the maximum speed rating shown on the nameplate.

## Best Practices
- Always quote the serial number from the nameplate for correspondence, spares orders, and accessories (see storage.md and spare_parts.md).
- Confirm ATEX classification matches the actual installation zone before commissioning in a hazardous area.

### FAQ
Q: What does the model code "80-50CPXS200" represent?
A: The first number (80) is the nominal suction size in mm, the second number (50) is the nominal discharge size in mm, the letters (CPXS) denote the configuration/model code, and the final number (200) is the nominal ISO maximum impeller diameter.

Q: What is the authoritative source for confirming pump size, serial number, and ATEX classification?
A: The equipment nameplate, always cross-checked against applicable certification documents.

Q: Does PUMP-101A use a mechanical shaft seal?
A: No — its magnetic drive design uses a containment shell between the magnets to prevent leakage, eliminating the need for a shaft seal.

Q: What is the maximum suction lift achievable with the self-priming configuration?
A: Up to 7 m (23 ft), using the reflux principle.
