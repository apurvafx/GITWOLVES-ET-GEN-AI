# Assembly Procedure

## Purpose
Describes the correct reassembly sequence for PUMP-101A after overhaul, to maintain bearing clearances, magnetic coupling integrity, and containment shell sealing.

## Engineering Information
Reassembly of PUMP-101A after overhaul must follow the correct sequence to maintain bearing clearances, magnetic coupling integrity, and containment shell sealing. Ensure all threads, gasket, and O-ring mating faces are clean, and apply thread sealant to non-face-sealing pipe thread fittings.

### Key Torque and Clearance Values
- Bearing end cover screws: 13 Nm (9.6 lbf·ft), crosswise.
- Outer magnet assembly: 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft), crosswise.
- Journal bearing clearance: 0.08–0.13 mm.
- Containment shell capscrews: 11 Nm, crosswise (with washers for PEEK shells).
- Bearing housing-to-cover bolts: 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft), crosswise.
- Inboard tolerance ring gap: minimum 1.5 mm between cut ends after trimming.
- Drive pin replacement threshold: replace if damaged or protruding more than 3 mm.
- Locknut/shim: 0.8 mm shim installed with inner rotor key.

## Procedure

### Outer Assembly – Bearing Housing
1. Replace the skid ring if removed.
2. Press new radial ball bearings onto the shaft (press on the inner race only, up to the shaft shoulders) if damaged.
3. Install the bearing/shaft assembly into the bearing housing bore.
4. Seat the end cover gasket and bolt the bearing end cover to the housing face; ensure oil return grooves on gasket and cover line up; tighten crosswise to 13 Nm (9.6 lbf·ft).
5. Confirm freedom of rotation, then install the flinger over the shaft (not pressed hard against the end cover).
6. Coat outer magnet rotor threads with anti-seize compound and screw into the bearing housing bore onto the frame shaft (right-hand thread).
7. Lock the rotor using a bar/screw through the housing hole, then torque the outer magnet assembly crosswise to 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft) using a coupling hub and key.
8. Remove the locking bar, confirm shaft rotates freely, and re-install the pipe plug.

### Inner Assembly – Casing Cover
1. Trim the inboard tolerance ring to length (remove 3 corrugations) and install into the inner bore, ensuring minimum 1.5 mm gap between cut ends.
2. Position the casing cover with "TOP" at 12 o'clock; install the front bearing bushing (lubrication groove at 9 o'clock) up to the shoulder.
3. Fit the bearing bush spacer if applicable, install the second tolerance ring, and insert the rear bearing bushing (lubrication groove at 6 o'clock).
4. Secure with radial socket head setscrews if a bearing bush spacer was fitted.

### Rotor Assembly
1. Clean and degrease impeller and shaft threads; apply anti-seize compound (copper-free) and install the impeller (right-hand thread), securing the shaft vertically in soft jaws.
2. Slide sleeves and spacer onto the shaft if fitted.
3. Slide the shaft through the casing cover (journal bearing clearance 0.08–0.13 mm — extreme care required).
4. Place the thrust collar gasket over the drive pin (replace pin if damaged or protruding more than 3 mm), install the thrust collar aligned with the drive pin.
5. Install the 0.8 mm shim, inner rotor key, and slide the inner rotor onto the shaft.
6. Thread the locknut onto the shaft (left-hand thread) and tighten to specified torque.
7. Check impeller clearance per the impeller clearance procedure.

### Containment Shell Assembly
- Single containment metallic: install shell gasket, metallic shell, backing ring, then tighten 6mm capscrews to 11 Nm crosswise.
- Single containment PEEK: install shell gasket, PEEK shell (with slotted temperature tap), tighten capscrews WITH WASHERS to 11 Nm crosswise (omitting washers causes excessive PEEK flange damage).
- Dual containment: install shell gasket, metallic shell, dual containment O-ring, then PEEK shell (pressure tap), tighten capscrews with washers to 11 Nm crosswise.

### Final Bearing Housing Assembly
1. Install jack bolts into the bearing housing and thread to the specified extension per frame size.
2. Apply liquid sealant (e.g., Hylomar Universal Blue) between bearing housing and casing cover gasket location.
3. Position the outer assembly over the shell until jacking screws contact the casing cover; back out jack screws alternately to fully seat the spigot.
4. Remove jacking bolts, install the four bearing housing-to-cover bolts, and torque crosswise to 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft).
5. Tighten foot bolts, recheck shaft rotation freedom, install and align the coupling per the alignment procedure.

## Inspection Points
- Freedom of shaft rotation after each major sub-assembly step.
- Journal bearing clearance (0.08–0.13 mm) during shaft installation.
- Drive pin condition (replace if damaged or protruding more than 3 mm).
- Correct orientation of "TOP" marking and lubrication grooves on bearing bushings.
- Tolerance ring cut-end gap (minimum 1.5 mm).
- Impeller clearance after rotor assembly (see impeller_clearance.md).

## Warnings
- On PEEK single containment shells, capscrews must be tightened WITH WASHERS — omitting washers causes excessive PEEK flange damage.
- Journal bearing clearance of 0.08–0.13 mm requires extreme care during shaft installation.
- Apply anti-seize compound (copper-free) to impeller and shaft threads before installation.
- Always confirm freedom of shaft rotation at each stage; binding indicates a misassembly that must be corrected before proceeding.
- Bolts must always be torqued crosswise on all bolting patterns — never in a circular sequence.

## Best Practices
- Ensure all threads, gasket, and O-ring mating faces are clean before assembly.
- Apply thread sealant to non-face-sealing pipe thread fittings.
- Back out jacking screws alternately to fully and evenly seat the spigot.
- Recheck shaft rotation freedom and coupling alignment as the final assembly step.

### FAQ
Q: Why must washers always be used when tightening PEEK containment shell capscrews?
A: Omitting washers causes excessive damage to the PEEK shell flange.

Q: What is the journal bearing clearance during shaft installation?
A: 0.08–0.13 mm, requiring extreme care during assembly.

Q: In what sequence should bearing housing-to-cover bolts be tightened?
A: Crosswise (star pattern) to 54 Nm (40 lbf·ft) — never in a circular sequence, which causes uneven gasket compression.

Q: What is the minimum tolerance ring cut-end gap after trimming?
A: 1.5 mm between the cut ends.
