# Electrical Connections

## Purpose
Defines electrical connection requirements, rotation-direction verification, regulatory considerations, and recommended protection systems for PUMP-101A.

## Engineering Information
Electrical connections for PUMP-101A must be made only by a qualified electrician, in compliance with relevant local, national, and international regulations.

### Requirements
- Follow the motor manufacturer's wiring instructions (normally supplied in the terminal box), including temperature protection, earth leakage protection, current protection, and other protective devices as appropriate.
- Check the nameplate to confirm the power supply matches equipment ratings.
- A device to provide emergency stopping must be fitted.
- If not pre-wired, controller/starter electrical details are supplied within the controller/starter enclosure; refer to the separate wiring diagram for pump sets with controllers.

### Regulatory Considerations
- For installations in potentially explosive atmospheres, comply with IEC 60079-14 for electrical connections in addition to general electrical codes.
- Be aware of EU Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC) requirements when wiring and installing equipment; ensure techniques used do not increase electromagnetic emissions or decrease immunity of the equipment or connected devices.

## Procedure

### Direction of Rotation Verification
1. The pump ships with the coupling element removed. Verify motor rotation direction BEFORE fitting the coupling element.
2. Confirm direction of rotation matches the direction arrow on the pump.
3. Never check rotation direction with the coupling spacer fitted — risk of severe contact between rotating and stationary components.
4. If site electrical work has been performed (e.g., supply phasing changed), re-check direction of rotation before restarting.

## Inspection Points
- Power supply rating versus nameplate rating.
- Direction of rotation versus the direction arrow on the pump (coupling spacer removed).
- Emergency stop device fitted and functional.
- Protective devices (temperature, earth leakage, current) installed per motor manufacturer wiring instructions.

## Warnings
- Never check rotation direction with the coupling spacer fitted — this risks severe contact between rotating and stationary components.
- Starting in reverse rotation will damage the pump.
- If site electrical work changes supply phasing, direction of rotation must be re-checked before restarting.
- All electrical connections must be made only by a qualified electrician, in compliance with relevant local, national, and international regulations.
- For potentially explosive atmospheres, electrical connections must comply with IEC 60079-14 in addition to general electrical codes.

## Best Practices
- Install a protection device (temperature monitor or power monitor) if the system could run the pump against a closed valve or below minimum continuous safe flow.
- Fit a power monitor to stop or prevent starting the pump if dry running or empty start-up is possible, particularly for flammable liquids.
- Install a leakage detection system if product leakage could create a hazard.
- Use temperature or vibration monitoring at bearings to prevent excessive surface temperatures.
- For dual containment shells, monitor the interspace pressure using a pressure switch connected to the motor starter or an alarm.

### FAQ
Q: When should motor rotation direction be verified?
A: Before fitting the coupling element, since the pump ships with the coupling element removed specifically to allow safe rotation checks.

Q: Why must rotation direction never be checked with the coupling spacer fitted?
A: Because it risks severe contact between rotating and stationary components if the rotation direction is incorrect.

Q: What happens if PUMP-101A is started in reverse rotation?
A: Starting in reverse rotation will damage the pump.

Q: What additional code applies to electrical connections in potentially explosive atmospheres?
A: IEC 60079-14, in addition to general local, national, and international electrical codes.
