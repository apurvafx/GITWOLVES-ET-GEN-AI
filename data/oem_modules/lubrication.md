# Lubrication

## Purpose
Defines oil and grease lubrication requirements, fill procedures, re-lubrication intervals, and bearing temperature limits for PUMP-101A to ensure reliable bearing life.

## Engineering Information

PUMP-101A bearings may be oil lubricated or grease lubricated. The bearing housing on oil lubricated units includes an oil sight glass or a constant level oiler bottle to maintain correct oil level. Grease lubricated bearing housings use sealed-for-life bearings and are supplied pre-greased.

### Oil Specification
- Recommended viscosity grades: ISO VG 32, ISO VG 46, ISO VG 68.
- Oil must be a high quality mineral oil with oxidation and foam inhibitors, or an approved synthetic oil.
- Oil temperature range depends on grade:
  - VG 32: -5 to 65°C
  - VG 46: -5 to 78°C
  - VG 68: -5 to 80°C
- For ambient temperatures below -5°C, ensure oil pour point is at least 15°C lower than ambient, or use SAE 5W-50 / API-SJ oil.
- Do not exceed the upper operating temperature range of the selected oil.

### Operating Parameters Summary
- Oil lubricated bearings: change every 4,000 operating hours or every 6 months (whichever is sooner); more frequent for hot service or damp/corrosive atmospheres.
- Grease lubricated bearings: sealed for life; renew every 12,000 running hours or every 2 years (whichever is sooner).
- Bearing temperature rise: up to 50°C above ambient permitted.
- Maximum bearing temperature: 82°C (180°F).

## Procedure

### Filling Procedure (Oil Lubricated Units)
1. Unscrew the oil filler/breather.
2. Fill through the orifice to the marked level (sight glass or constant level oiler bottle).
3. For constant level oilers, unscrew or hinge back the transparent bottle, fill with oil, and refit upright. Repeat until oil remains visible in the bottle.
4. Oil lubricated units are shipped WITHOUT oil and must be filled before starting the pump.

## Inspection Points
- Oil level and condition (sight glass / constant level oiler bottle).
- Running hours since last grease recharge (grease lubricated units).
- Bearing temperature trend versus benchmark reading.

## Warnings
- Oil lubricated units are shipped WITHOUT oil — running dry on start-up will damage the bearings.
- Bearing temperature must never exceed 82°C (180°F).
- A continuously rising or abruptly rising bearing temperature indicates a fault and must be investigated immediately.
- Do not exceed the upper operating temperature range of the selected oil grade.

## Best Practices
- Use lubricant and bearing temperature trend analysis to optimize change intervals.
- Pumps handling high temperature liquids may require bearing cooling to stay within temperature limits.
- Increase oil change frequency for hot service or damp/corrosive atmospheres.

### FAQ
Q: What oil viscosity grade should be used for PUMP-101A?
A: ISO VG 32, VG 46, or VG 68, selected according to expected operating and ambient temperature range.

Q: How often should oil lubricated bearings be changed?
A: Every 4,000 operating hours or at least every 6 months, whichever occurs first, with more frequent changes for hot, damp, or corrosive service.

Q: Does PUMP-101A ship with oil already in the bearing housing?
A: No. Oil lubricated units are shipped without oil and must be filled before starting the pump.

Q: What is the maximum allowable bearing temperature?
A: 82°C (180°F). Bearing temperature may normally rise up to 50°C above ambient, but a continuous or abrupt rise beyond that indicates a fault.
