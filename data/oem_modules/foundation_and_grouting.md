# Foundation and Grouting

## Purpose
Describes foundation location and preparation requirements, and grouting practice, for correctly installing PUMP-101A to prevent vibration transmission, lateral movement, and premature alignment failure.

## Engineering Information
Correct foundation preparation and grouting for PUMP-101A prevents vibration transmission, lateral movement, and premature alignment failure. Non-compliance may void the warranty.

### Location
The pump should be located to allow room for access, ventilation, maintenance, and inspection, with ample headroom for lifting, and positioned as close as practicable to the liquid supply. Refer to the general arrangement drawing for the specific pump set.

### Foundation Requirements
- The baseplate must be mounted on a firm foundation — either appropriate-thickness quality concrete or a sturdy steel framework. It should NOT be distorted or pulled down onto the foundation surface, but supported to maintain original alignment.
- Install the baseplate onto packing pieces evenly spaced adjacent to foundation bolts.
- Level with shims between baseplate and packing pieces.
- Although the pump and driver are aligned before dispatch, alignment must be checked — if incorrect, this indicates a twisted baseplate requiring re-shimming.
- If not supplied, guarding must be fitted per ISO 12100 and EN953.

## Procedure

### Grouting
1. Grout the foundation bolts where applicable.
2. After completing pipework connections and rechecking coupling alignment, grout the baseplate per good engineering practice.
3. Fabricated steel, folded steel, and cast iron baseplates can be filled with grout.
4. Foundation bolts should only be fully tightened after the grout has cured.

### Part Assemblies
On baseplated pump sets, coupling elements are supplied loose. The installer is responsible for final alignment of the pump set as detailed in the alignment procedure.

## Inspection Points
- Baseplate flatness/twist (verified via alignment check after installation).
- Foundation bolt tightness (only after grout has cured).
- Guarding installed per ISO 12100 / EN953 if not supplied.
- Coupling alignment rechecked after pipework connections, before grouting.

## Warnings
- Polycrete baseplates CANNOT be grouted using standard grouting practice — refer to the separate polycrete baseplate installation manual.
- The baseplate should NOT be distorted or pulled down onto the foundation surface; doing so disturbs alignment.
- Foundation bolts should only be fully tightened after the grout has cured — premature tightening can distort the baseplate.

## Best Practices
- Grouting provides solid contact between the pump unit and foundation, prevents lateral movement of vibrating equipment, and dampens resonant vibrations.
- Recheck coupling alignment after completing pipework connections and before final grouting.
- If alignment is found incorrect after installation despite factory alignment, treat this as an indicator of a twisted baseplate requiring re-shimming rather than adjusting only the coupling.

### FAQ
Q: Can polycrete baseplates be grouted using the standard grouting procedure?
A: No. Polycrete baseplates cannot be grouted; refer to the separate polycrete baseplate installation manual.

Q: When should foundation bolts be fully tightened?
A: Only after the grout has cured.

Q: What does incorrect alignment after installation typically indicate?
A: A twisted baseplate requiring re-shimming, since the pump and driver are aligned before dispatch.

Q: What baseplate materials can be filled with grout?
A: Fabricated steel, folded steel, and cast iron baseplates.
