# Priming and Auxiliary Supplies

## Purpose
Describes correct priming and filling procedures for standard and self-priming PUMP-101A configurations, and auxiliary supply requirements, to prevent dry running and seizure.

## Engineering Information
PUMP-101A must never be run dry. Correct priming before continuous operation is essential to prevent seizure of the magnetic drive and impeller.

### Initial Fill Quantities by Pump Size (Self-Priming Models)
| Pump Size | Initial Fill |
|---|---|
| 40-40 (125 frame) | 2.5 L (0.65 US gal) |
| 80-80 (125 frame) | 6.0 L (1.60 US gal) |

- Self-priming action works on the reflux principle for suction lifts up to 7 m (23 ft); a separate air pump is not normally required.

## Procedure

### Standard (Non-Self-Priming) Filling and Priming
1. Ensure the inlet pipe and pump casing are completely full of liquid before starting continuous duty operation.
2. Priming may be performed with an ejector, vacuum pump interceptor, other equipment, or by flooding from the inlet source.
3. Pumps with foot valves in service may be primed by passing liquid back from the outlet pipe through the pump.

### Self-Priming Models — Filling
1. Fill the pump with the liquid to be pumped (or a compatible liquid) via the filling hole before starting continuous duty operation.
2. When the initial fill reaches the suction pipe, excess liquid flows out of the casing, indicating correct fill level.

### Priming Air Venting (Self-Priming Discharge Pipework)
The delivery pipework must permit priming air to escape unhindered without back pressure and prevent excessive run-back of liquid on shutdown (to minimize syphoning). Options:
1. Partly open the discharge regulating valve during priming to vent air freely.
2. Fit an automatic air release valve on the discharge pipework (only if released gases are environmentally safe).
3. Run an air bleed pipe from the discharge pipework back to the suction tank/sump (requires manual/automatic control to prevent continuous re-circulation).

### Auxiliary Supplies
Ensure all electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, sealant, and lubrication systems (as applicable) are connected and operational before starting the pump.

## Inspection Points
- Inlet pipe and pump casing completely full of liquid before starting (non-self-priming models).
- Correct initial fill level confirmed by liquid flowing out of the casing (self-priming models).
- Priming air venting arrangement functioning (self-priming discharge pipework).
- All auxiliary supplies connected and operational before starting.

## Warnings
- PUMP-101A must never be run dry — dry running can quickly seize the magnetic drive and impeller.
- Automatic air release valves on discharge pipework must only be used if released gases are environmentally safe.
- Air bleed pipes back to the suction tank/sump require manual or automatic control to prevent continuous re-circulation.

## Best Practices
- Confirm all electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, sealant, and lubrication auxiliary systems are connected and operational before starting the pump.
- On self-priming models, verify correct initial fill by observing excess liquid flow out of the casing once the fill reaches the suction pipe.

### FAQ
Q: Why should PUMP-101A never run dry?
A: Dry running can quickly seize the magnetic drive and impeller, causing severe damage within minutes.

Q: How is correct initial fill level confirmed on self-priming models?
A: When the initial fill reaches the suction pipe, excess liquid flows out of the casing, indicating the correct fill level has been reached.

Q: What is the maximum suction lift achievable by the self-priming reflux principle?
A: Up to 7 m (23 ft), without normally requiring a separate air pump.

Q: What methods can be used to prime a standard (non-self-priming) pump?
A: An ejector, a vacuum pump interceptor, flooding from the inlet source, or (for pumps with foot valves) passing liquid back from the outlet pipe through the pump.
